Maximize Your Professional Potential with Continuing Education Credits

Albert Einstein once said, “Life is like riding a bicycle. To keep your balance, you must keep moving.” To get the most out of life, you must always move forward and challenge yourself to grow. Therefore, continuing your education is important, and the most successful professionals are usually the ones who never stop learning.


When it comes to positive professional growth, earning continuing education credits (CEC) is a perfect way to make an impact. Whether you’re looking to maintain your license by adding required courses or simply hoping to strengthen your skills, continuing education credits can help you become more a well-rounded professional. Here are answers to common CEC questions to help create the best possible pathway for your professional success.


What are Continuing Education Credits?

Continuing Education Credits are post-secondary education opportunities such as degree programs, online courses, career training, and more. These helpful credits can make a difference in careers in a variety of fields. They add value to your resume and your experiences by keeping you up to date on the current and emerging best practices, processes, technologies, and methodologies.


Some professional careers, such as IT, require you to take CECs to maintain your industry certification. You could consider them an investment toward a more successful future.

CECs are not academic credit and are not given for academic research. This differentiates them from other academic accreditations. In addition, CECs are approved by a governing body and meet certain requirements and a certain number of credit hours.
